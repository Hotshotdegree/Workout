Training Principles

One True Rest Day

Cardiovascular fitness adapts in days. Tendons and bones take weeks. Your rest days protect your shins, knees, and elbows — the things that sideline you.

Run Intervals Logic

Week 1–2: 90s run / 2min walk × 8–10 rounds. Progress only when HR stays out of Zone 5 for all rounds and breathing feels controlled. Don't rush it.

Run to Park Rule

On push/pull days, run to park at Zone 2 only — it's a warm-up, not a workout. Save effort for the bar. Run home easy or walk if needed.

Zone Targets

Zone 2–3: most of your running. Zone 4: short hard intervals only. Zone 5: avoid. Target avg HR 140–150 bpm for most sessions.

Pull-Up Rule

Never go to failure on pull-ups. Stop 1–2 reps short every set. This builds sustainable strength and keeps your elbows healthy long-term.

Form Over Reps

Full range of motion always. Controlled descent on every push-up and pull-up. A perfect 6 builds more than a sloppy 12.

Running Form

Short quick steps. Land under your body. Shoulders relaxed. Arms forward/back not across. Chest tall. These fix 90% of beginner running pain.

Post-Run Routine

Every run: calf stretch 30s each, hip flexor stretch 30s each, hamstring stretch 30s, tibialis raises × 20. 6 minutes. Non-negotiable.

Breathing

Rhythmic: inhale 3 steps, exhale 2. Or inhale 2, exhale 2. Nasal breathing on easy portions to self-regulate effort. Diaphragm, not chest.

Finish Feeling

"I could have done one more interval." This is the golden rule. If you finish gassed, you went too hard. Aerobic base = consistency over intensity.

Electrolytes

After sweaty sessions: 500ml water, ¼ tsp salt, 1 tsp honey, ½ tbsp lemon. Beats most commercial drinks and costs almost nothing.

Warning Signs

Sharp shin pain or pain along the inner shin bone = stop running. Dull calf/quad tightness = normal early adaptation. Know the difference.
